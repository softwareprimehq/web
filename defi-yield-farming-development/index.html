What is DeFi Yield Farming?

DeFi Yield Farming is a technique where users earn more returns by providing liquidity to decentralized platforms. By staking digital assets in liquidity pools, users can generate returns in the form of tokens or shares of trading fees, It maximizes their crypto assets.

DeFi Yield Farming - Here the word “farming” refers to providing liquidity to different DeFi protocols. In exchange for your contribution, the platforms issue LP tokens that represent their share in the liquidity pool. These LP tokens can be staked on protocols to earn the platform’s native tokens, also these tokens can be used on other platforms or reinvested for compounding interest. This whole system works on blockchain-based smart contracts that handle crypto financial activities and token provision automatically.

DeFi Yield Farming Working Steps

How does Yield Farming Work?

A Clear Explanation of How Yield Farming Operates

Step 1

Liquidity providers offer their tokens into the liquidity pool, and smart contracts handle that fund securely. Additionally, the DeFi platform verifies users' funds through a secure process for an extra safety layer for all participants.

Step 2

Liquidity providers are offered LP tokens in return for locking up their digital assets into liquidity pools based on the amount they deposited. This encourages users to support liquidity for smoother trading operations.

Step 3

The earned tokens from liquidity pools can be reinvested into Farming pools to explore additional earning opportunities. This strategy allows liquidity providers to diversify their investments and optimize overall returns.

ROI Calculation For DeFi Yield Farming

How to Calculate Returns on Yield Farming?

Total value locked (TVL)

TVL shows the total amount of funds locked in the platform’s liquidity pools. A higher TVL shows a larger user base and a more robust earning opportunity for liquidity providers.

Annual Percentage Yield (APY)

APY gives the annualized interest rate by taking compound interest into account. Compound interest differs from simple interest because it’s calculated periodically, and the new amount is instantly added to your balance.

Annual Percentage Rate (APR)

APR indicates the yearly interest rate on the yield farming investments but excludes compounding. It shows as a percentage and represents the income you gain from an investment in a year.
