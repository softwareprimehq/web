A Quick Comparison

Exploring Different Fundraising Models

Key Differences Between IDOs, IEOs, ICOs, and STOs

Offering Type Participants Regulation Trading Setup
Initial DEX Offerings (IDOs) Anyone with a crypto wallet can participate. Barely regulated, with minimal oversight. Tokens available to trade immediately after the sale. Low fees, fast setup, integration with DEXs like Uniswap.
Initial Exchange Offerings (IEOs) Account on exchange + KYC required. Tightly regulated with exchange oversight. Tokens traded on the exchange after the sale. High, fees, quick setup, integration with CEXs like Binance.
Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs) Need a crypto wallet + KYC checks. Minimal regulation, but growing oversight. Tokens may become available after a brief waiting period. Swift setup, typically unregulated, with low fees.
Security Token Offerings (STOs) Accredited investors participate in STOs on regulated platforms. Strictly regulated to meet legal requirements. Tokens face regulatory delays and limited liquidity. Higher costs and slower set up due to legal requirements.
